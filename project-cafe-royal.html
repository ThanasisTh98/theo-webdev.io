#project-overview

Design Challenge

Cafe Royal is a very real cafe owned by my uncle and currently managed by my cousin. As it stands currently their website is ancient and hasn't been updated since the early 2000s, making it difficult for customers to find information about the place.

I have offered to redesign the website to improve user experience and modernize the brand, for FREE! Cause I love my family (and also give me good experience working with clients with no additional risk or stress).

The goal is to create a website that not only looks great but is also easy to use and provides all the necessary information to customers.

Design Solution

I created a comprehensive design system in Figma that matches the colour scheme of Cafe Royal, incorporating bright reds and blocky typography to reflect the brand's identity.

The design is extremely user-friendly, with a focus on intuitive navigation and easy access to information.

Key features include a detailed menu section, links to third-party ordering platforms like Deliveroo and Uber Eats, a store locator, and a contact form. The design is fully responsive, ensuring a seamless experience across all devices.

Design Goals Achieved

  • Intuitive user experience flow
  • Brand-consistent visual identity
  • Accessible design principles
  • Mobile-first approach

#all-designs

I went through a total of 4 iterations and about 10 prototypes before arriving at the final design, Experiment 04

Original Homepage

The outdated homepage from the early 2000s showing poor navigation and dated design. This was the starting point that clearly needed a complete overhaul to meet modern web standards.

Key issues included outdated fonts, poor color contrast, non-responsive layout, cluttered information architecture, and no clear call-to-action buttons. The site lacked any modern UX principles and failed to represent the quality of the actual cafe experience.

Most importantly, it wasn't mobile-friendly at all, which is crucial since most customers would be searching for cafe information on their phones while on the go.

First 3 Experiments

New colour scheme, new logo, improved layout. However it was giving early 2010s website which I didn't like, it needed a modern twist. These experiments helped establish the direction but weren't quite there yet.

I introduced the signature red color palette that matches the cafe's branding, redesigned the logo to be more contemporary, and restructured the navigation to be more intuitive. The typography was upgraded to modern web fonts, and I added proper spacing and visual hierarchy.

While these changes were a massive improvement from the original, the overall aesthetic still felt dated - reminiscent of websites from 2010-2012. The design lacked the clean, minimalist approach that modern users expect. It was clear I needed to push further toward contemporary design trends.

These iterations were crucial learning experiences that helped me understand what worked (the color scheme, improved navigation) and what needed refinement (overall visual style, component design, user interface patterns).

My Final Design

There were a few more designs in between the last 3 and this. I wanted to achieve a more modern and user-friendly interface, so I incorporated a very clean and minimalistic design approach. By far my favourite design with new logos.

This final iteration embraces modern design principles with generous white space, subtle shadows, and a refined color palette. I completely redesigned the logo to be more contemporary and versatile across different mediums. The navigation is intuitive with clear visual hierarchy and improved accessibility.

Key improvements include a mobile-first responsive design, optimized loading times, strategic use of the brand's red color for call-to-action elements, and a component-based design system that ensures consistency across all pages.

The result is a website that truly represents the quality and warmth of Cafe Royal while providing customers with an effortless digital experience. This design successfully bridges the gap between the cafe's traditional charm and modern digital expectations.
